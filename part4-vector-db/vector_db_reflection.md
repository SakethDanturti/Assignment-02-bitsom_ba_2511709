## Vector DB Use Case

A traditional keyword-based database search would not be sufficient on its own for this use case. Legal contracts are long, complex, and often written in highly formal language. A lawyer may ask, “What are the termination clauses?” but the contract might use very different wording such as “grounds for cancellation,” “right to dissolve the agreement,” or “conditions under which either party may end this contract.” A keyword search would mainly look for exact or closely matching terms, so it could miss relevant sections when the wording differs. It may also return too many irrelevant matches where the word appears without answering the real question.

A vector database would be much more useful because it supports semantic search. In this system, the 500-page contract would first be split into smaller chunks, and each chunk would be converted into an embedding, which is a numerical representation of meaning. The lawyer’s question would also be converted into an embedding. The vector database would then find the chunks whose meanings are most similar to the question, even if they do not contain the same exact words.

This makes the search much more intelligent and context-aware. Instead of matching only keywords, it retrieves the most relevant legal passages based on intent and meaning. In practice, the best system would combine both approaches: keyword search for exact terms and vector search for semantic understanding. The vector database would therefore act as the core retrieval layer that helps the system surface the right clauses quickly and accurately.
